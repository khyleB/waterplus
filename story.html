History

Initiated by John Odolon and Kevin Best but under implementation by the Odenaansan Project. John and Kevin met through a mutual friend and were called to work together on the application of the ICD approach in the global south. As this work is community centred, we consulted the traditional leadership of Agereger and they pointed to the water problem.

The Story of WaterPlus

Odenaansan & WaterPlus

WaterPlus is built on the principles of the Odenaansan Integrated Community Development (ICD) approach, which emphasizes community-led initiatives and holistic development. By adopting this approach, WaterPlus prioritizes the unique needs and aspirations of the communities we serve, integrating clean water access with broader development goals. This comprehensive strategy enables us to address the root causes of water scarcity, promote sustainable livelihoods, and foster resilient communities and our practical story begins in Agereger.

Water Scarcity in Agereger

Agereger, a rural village located at the headwaters of the Nile River is a community struggling to access clean water. The people of Agereger face a daily challenge of walking long distances to access clean water. Women and children trek for miles, often under the scorching sun, to fetch water from distant sources. This exhausting task not only takes a toll on their health but also steals precious time from education, work, and family. Our team was moved by the resilience and determination of the Agereger community, and by working closely with local leaders and residents, we designed and implemented a sustainable water solution.

The Future of WaterPlus

We have plans of setting up solar-powered pumping boreholes in the road to promote a sustainable community. We hope through our initiatives to bring new life to Agereger. Children attend school instead of walking for water. Women start businesses and improve their families' health thereby making the community flourish. Agereger's story inspired us to expand our mission to three more Iteso communities within the neighboring countries. We're dedicated to creating communities that are self-reliant and self-developed under the integrated community development of the Odenaansan organization.
